The problem every SOC recognises

Security operations centres are built on a paradox. Organisations invest heavily in detections — endpoint telemetry, identity signals, cloud events, network flows — yet the sheer volume of alerts generated ensures that most of them receive a cursory glance before being closed or escalated. Analyst burnout, high turnover, and the expertise gap in specialist markets like Singapore and ASEAN compound the challenge.

The traditional response to alert fatigue has been to tune thresholds, add more playbooks, or expand the team. These are necessary — but they do not scale. What scales is intelligence. What scales is reasoning at machine speed without sacrificing the quality of human judgment.

"The most dangerous alerts are not the ones that fire loudly. They are the ones that look routine until someone with the right context looks closely."

— Common thread in CISO conversations across APJ financial services and government sectors

What Purple AI Auto-Investigations actually does

Auto-Investigations is a capability within Purple AI — SentinelOne's generative AI security analyst embedded natively into the Singularity Platform. It does not just surface data. It reasons through it.

When an analyst opens the Singularity Ops Center UAM (User Activity Monitoring) page, a single click triggers a full automated investigation. From that moment, Purple AI operates like a senior tier-2 analyst who never sleeps:

Step 1 — Contextual data collection

Purple AI autonomously pulls relevant telemetry across endpoint, identity, cloud, and network — without the analyst having to specify what to look for.

Step 2 — Dynamic reasoning plan

Trained alongside SentinelOne's Wayfinder MDR team, it constructs an investigation plan that mirrors expert analyst logic — and adapts the plan as new evidence emerges.

Step 3 — Cross-signal correlation

Evidence is correlated across data sources, lateral movement paths are traced, and attack timelines are reconstructed with full provenance.

Step 4 — Verdict with justification

A structured report is generated — executive summary, attack timeline, remediation steps, and a detailed verdict justification. Every conclusion is explainable.

Step 5 — Automated response (optional)

AI Verdicts can trigger Singularity HyperAutomation flows — governed, policy-driven response across the security ecosystem at machine speed.

Why this matters for enterprise security teams

There is a meaningful difference between AI-assisted triage and AI-driven investigation. Assisted triage gives an analyst a better-organised queue. Driven investigation gives an analyst a completed case file. Auto-Investigations delivers the latter.

The MDR-trained difference

What separates Auto-Investigations from generic AI triage tools is its lineage. The reasoning model was developed in direct collaboration with SentinelOne's Wayfinder MDR team — the analysts who respond to real incidents across thousands of customer environments globally. This means the investigation logic reflects actual adversary behaviour, real escalation patterns, and the decision trees that experienced practitioners use under pressure.

When the AI says a chain of events represents lateral movement rather than routine admin activity, it is not pattern-matching against a signature database. It is applying the same reasoning a seasoned MDR analyst would apply — and explaining precisely why it reached that conclusion.

"Explainability is not a nice-to-have. In regulated markets — FSI in Singapore, government in ASEAN, healthcare across the region — every verdict that drives a response action needs to be justified to regulators, auditors, and the board. Auto-Investigations produces that justification by design."

What this means for CISOs and security leadership

From an operational standpoint, Auto-Investigations addresses three distinct pain points that come up consistently in CISO conversations across the region:

  • 1
    Analyst capacity and retention When L1 and L2 analysts are not spending hours on repetitive triage, they focus on high-value work — threat hunting, detection engineering, adversary intelligence. This matters enormously for talent retention in markets where security specialists are chronically in short supply.
  • 2
    Governance and accountability Every Auto-Investigation produces a documented audit trail — what data was analysed, what inferences were drawn, what verdict was reached, and what actions were taken. This is the governance layer that regulators increasingly expect from AI-assisted security operations.
  • 3
    Mean time to respond (MTTR) The gap between alert generation and response action is where breaches expand. Auto-Investigations compresses that gap from hours to minutes, with HyperAutomation extending the reach into actual remediation — containment, isolation, credential revocation — governed by policy.

Privacy-first AI in regulated environments

A critical architecture point worth calling out explicitly: Purple AI Auto-Investigations operates on a privacy-first model. SentinelOne does not use customer data to train its models. For organisations operating under PDPA in Singapore, under Australia's Privacy Act, or under sector-specific regimes like MAS TRM or APRA CPS 234, this is a foundational requirement — not a differentiator.

The combination of strict data sovereignty controls, human-in-the-loop governance checkpoints, and configurable scope boundaries means Auto-Investigations can be deployed in air-gapped, sovereign, and highly regulated environments without compromise.
